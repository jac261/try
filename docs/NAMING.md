# Naming the app: the finalist board

For Jack. "Try" has done its job as a working name, but it fails as a public
one, and the rename is cheap now and expensive after beta: the name decides
the domain, the domain decides production Clerk, and wave one's home screens
and word of mouth lock it in. This doc is the full search, receipts included,
so the remaining work is taste, not research.

## Why not just keep Try

Screened as the control alongside everything else. Searching "try app"
surfaces TriDot, MOTTIV and TRIQ, not us: as a bare dictionary word it is
unsearchable and effectively untrademarkable. try.com sits on a marketplace
with a likely five to six figure ask. It also fails the pub test in its own
way ("the app's called Try" invites "called what?" every time).

## Criteria (priority order)

1. Pub test: heard aloud once at a club session, spelled right that evening.
2. Signal: triathlon vocabulary, the calm-coach voice, or an earned meaning.
3. Searchable: the name plus "app" must be a winnable query.
4. Domain: a sane .com, or a clean .app or .cc, plausibly available.
5. Clear of the endurance-app namespace (Runna, TriDot, MOTTIV, TrainerRoad,
   TrainingPeaks, Humango, JOIN, Strava, Zwift, WHOOP, Athletica) and of the
   wellness and meditation cluster.
6. Short enough to sit under a home-screen icon.

## Process

Five generation-and-screening passes plus ad-hoc screens of Jon's candidates:
roughly 140 names generated across directions (tri vocabulary, tri and try
root inventions, calm-coach words, evocative real words, foreign endurance
words, descriptive compounds, insight and self-knowledge, emergence and
becoming). Every candidate screened with receipts: web searches for app,
fitness and brand collisions; DNS probes on .com, .app, .cc and get- and
hq- variants; trademark red flags from search results. Survivors judged by
three personas per pass, always including a sceptical co-founder proxy after
round one's tri-prefix shortlist (Trisage, Tridian, Tryzen) was rejected as
pharma-sounding.

## The finalists

| Name | Flavour | Screen | Domain path | Main risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Taper | earned tri vocabulary | clear | taper.app unregistered | reads as one training phase |
| Nascor | becoming (Latin, "I am born") | clear | full sweep: .com, .app, .cc all open | NASCAR echo said aloud |
| Sohatsu | emergence (Japanese, 創発) | clear | .app, .cc, getsohatsu.com open | three syllables, brand must teach the word |
| Mieru | insight (Japanese, "to see clearly") | clear | .cc, getmieru.com open | pronunciation friction, niche dev-tool namesake |

**Taper.** Real vocabulary the audience already owns, and it describes what
the adaptive engine actually does: easing load at the right moment. The only
trademark holder anywhere is an industrial bearings company from an unrelated
class. Highest consensus score of the whole exercise (8.3), first pick of two
judges in its round. The dissent: it names the pre-race phase, which could
read narrow for a product that also covers base and build. Counterpoint: the
taper is the part of training athletes love.

**Nascor.** Latin "I am born." The only candidate in about 140 with the bare
.com, the .app and the .cc all genuinely unregistered, and no fitness-space
collision at all (nearest namesakes are small IT firms). Scored 8.0 with all
three judges voting. The one flaw every judge flagged: one beat from NASCAR
when spoken. Say it aloud a few times before deciding; a British club
audience barely thinks about NASCAR, but it is real.

**Sohatsu.** Japanese 創発, the actual term for emergence in the
complex-systems sense, which is not a bolted-on metaphor here: the engine's
whole premise is that fitness emerges from simple daily signals honestly
read. Empty namespace (no product, app, gym or brand found anywhere), open
.app and .cc. Costs: three syllables, a mild spelling risk after one hearing
(sohatsu vs souhatsu), and the Strava trade: the brand teaches the word.

**Mieru.** Japanese "to become visible, to see clearly": what the readiness
engine does every morning. Only clear survivor of the insight pass. Costs: a
pronunciation hurdle (mee-eh-roo), the bare .com parked in China, mieru.app
held by an unrelated Japanese product, and a niche developer-tool namesake
some of our data-literate audience may know.

**Runner-up.** Quiet Engine (7.2, clear screen): the one compound that reads
as a voice rather than a feature list. Two words and a squatted bare .com
keep it off the main board.

## Notable kills, so nobody relitigates them

- **Kensho** (Jon liked the meaning): a live Kensho Premium Fitness app on
  both stores plus S&P's Kensho Technologies on the flagship domain. Dead.
- **Monarch** (the butterfly idea): five live fitness products on the exact
  name, Monarch Money owning the search results, Monarch Airlines in British
  memory, and Monark ergometers inside sports science itself. Dead.
- **Naru** (Jon loved it; 成る, "to become"): Naro AI Fitness Coach is one
  letter away with essentially our pitch (HRV, sleep, recovery, adaptive
  coaching), plus scattered Naru fitness businesses and no domain path. A
  full variant screen (Narou, Nareru, Inaru, Minaru, Kanaru, Naruka, Naruma,
  Sonaru) found no clean survivor: Kanaru best dodges the Naro sound but has
  a live identical-name app; Minaru is collision-clean but domain-blocked;
  Naruka collides with NetEase's Naraka: Bladepoint; Sonaru is a live app
  name and mishears as "sonar". The becoming-meaning survives in Sohatsu
  and Nascor instead.
- **Tojo**: dead on connotation for a British audience before screening even
  starts.
- **Steady**: the best emotional fit of any real word, but a live gym-log app
  with an adaptive-suggestion pitch owns the space, plus a funded
  subscription-platform brand.
- **Brick**: authentic tri vocabulary, crowded general-fitness namespace, no
  domain path. **Slipstream**: collides with a swim-training machine our
  exact audience knows. **Taper aside, the round-one tri-prefix inventions**
  (Trisage, Tridian, Tryzen, Trydo, Trivance): rejected on taste, they read
  as pharma or CMS brands.

## Decision path

1. Pick a flavour: earned vocabulary (Taper), ownable becoming (Nascor or
   Sohatsu), or insight (Mieru). Saying each aloud in a sentence ("I log it
   in ...", "have you tried ...") beats staring at the table.
2. Live with the pick for a week. If it still fits, register the domains the
   same day (all four have paths under about 20 pounds except negotiations).
3. Then the rename bundle, in order: domain, production Clerk instance,
   PWA manifest and icon, backend CORS and JWT config, README and repo
   naming. One evening of work once the name is fixed.
