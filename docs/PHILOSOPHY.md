# The Try training philosophy

The plan serves the athlete's body, not the other way round.

Most training plans are contracts written weeks in advance to a body that
does not exist yet. Try treats the plan as a hypothesis: this morning's
readiness, this week's ramp and this block's form are constantly allowed to
revise it. Readiness and the fitness/fatigue picture are the heart of the
machine, and sessions change based on what they say.

Everything else follows from that.

## Consistency beats heroics

The ramp guardrail is the clearest expression: the app will actively take
training away when fitness grows too fast, because the evidence says builds
fail from rate, not volume. The load curve opens moderately for the same
reason, recovery weeks are a non-negotiable rhythm, and a stalled build gets
a catch-up rather than a shrug. Showing up steadily is the thing being
optimised.

## Propose, never impose

The engine never silently rewrites anything. Every adaptation arrives as one
tap-to-accept suggestion with its reasoning attached, wears a visible tag
(Eased, Trimmed, Boosted) and can be undone. The athlete stays the coach's
boss. And it is one voice at a time: five simultaneous alerts is noise, and
noise gets ignored.

## Hard things are protected, not softened

Race day never moves. A benchmark test caught on a bad morning is
rescheduled, never diluted, because a compromised test poisons every pace
downstream. When the engine eases a day it swaps intensity for aerobic
volume rather than deleting training. The app makes training sustainable,
not easy.

## Honest numbers, honestly framed

Zones anchor to the athlete's own measured numbers and admit it when they
are estimates. Tests exist to turn guesses into measurements. The fitness
watcher says when reality has drifted from the plan in either direction,
including when the targets are set too high. And the support copy refuses to
gamify recovery as weakness: a negative ramp bar is not failure.

## Simple by default, deep on demand

One score in the morning, one session today, one suggestion at a time, with
every chart, threshold and rationale one "What is this?" away for the
athlete who wants to understand. Trust the summary or interrogate the
machinery; both are first-class.

## Fitness is for something

The plan always points somewhere: a race, arrived at fresh, via the taper's
deliberate letting-go. When there is no race, maintenance keeps the engine
warm on purpose rather than pretending every week is a build. Even the
durability work carries the philosophy: races are won in the last hour, so
the hard intervals come at the end of long sessions, on tired legs, where
race day actually lives.

## In one sentence

An evidence-based coach that adapts the plan to your body every morning,
protects you from your own enthusiasm, and always tells you why.
